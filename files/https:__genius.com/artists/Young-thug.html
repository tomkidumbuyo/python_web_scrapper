Young Thug

AKA: Young Thug & Young Stoner Life Records, Big Slime, Jeffery Williams, Jeffery L. Williams, Jeffery, King Slime, Thugger, SEX, and Jeffery Lamar Williams

About Young Thug

Jeffery Lamar Williams (b. August 16, 1991), better known by his stage name Young Thug, is an innovative trap artist from Atlanta who has shown his versatility from his signature off-the-wall flows over hard trap beats to experimental crooning over poppier beats. His rise to popularity began after being taken under Gucci Mane’s wing in 2013, when he released his first major hit, “Stoner,” which went viral in social media videos. His follow-up single, “Danny Glover,” was received with critical acclaim and saw remixes from various rappers, the most popular one made by Nicki Minaj. He continued this streak after leaving Gucci’s 1017 Records in 2014 when he was featured on multiple high-profile singles, including T.I.’s “About The Money,” Tyga’s “Hookah,” and Rich Gang’s “Lifestyle.” On August 16, 2019, eight years after the release of his debut mixtape, Thug would go on to finally release his debut album, So Much Fun.

Since 2014, Thug’s career has been littered with leaks, as fans were able to hear over 80 leaked songs in 2015 (many of which were later re-released on Slime Season and its sequel), then almost twice that many leaks each in 2018 and 2019. However, despite all these leaks, Thug has still managed to put out almost exclusively unheard material on all of his projects since 2016’s I’m Up, consistently releasing three projects a year.

After Thug’s 2015 single “Best Friend” went platinum, “pick up the phone” became his highest-charting song as lead artist, peaking at 43 on Billboard’s Hot 100. This would later be succeeded by “Havana,” his collaboration with Camila Cabello that would top Billboard’s Hot 100 in 2018. However, his current highest-charting song as lead artist is “The London,” which debuted at 12 on the Hot 100.

At the 2019 Grammy Awards, Young Thug won his first Grammys for his work on Childish Gambino’s “This Is America” where he won awards for both Record & Song Of The Year.

Thug has been cited as an inspiration for many popular artists, including Lil Uzi Vert, Travis Scott, Gunna, Lil Baby, Juice WRLD, NBA YoungBoy, Lil Nas X, and even the legendary Kanye West. In 2016, Kanye revealed that he wanted to put out a collaborative project with Thugger, saying he’s like Bob Marley and featuring him on his 2016 album The Life of Pablo for “Highlights.”

While many have dubbed Thug as a huge inspiration, Jeffery himself consistently cites Lil Wayne as his biggest idol and influence, even though they have both publicly spoken about their issues with one another.

Young Thug’s frequent collaborators include Gunna, Migos, Travis Scott, Future, and Gucci Mane. Thug has also worked extensively with producers such as Wheezy, London On Da Track, Metro Boomin, TM88, and Southside.
